17.Adonis Blue

Adonis Blue (Polyommatus bellargus) is one of many types of butterflies that live in limestone grasslands in England. They have bright blue wings, while most females are dark brown. Their unique traits are thin, dark strips that line the tips of their wings, along with white edges. Adonis Blueâ€™s wingspan is about 1.5 inches (38 mm). Their colonies reach the peak of their numbers during summer. Adonis Blue was previously declined in numbers, but they have been multiplied to the point that they are no longer threatened.
